Additional Resources

The Democratizing Data initiative has a number of associated publications, reports, and additional resources that provide additional information on our guiding principles and processes.

Continuing Implementation of the Foundations for Evidence-Based Policymaking Act of 2018: Who Is Using the Data?

Nancy Potok

In this Effective Policy Learning piece, the author, Nancy Potok, a Visiting Fellow at RTI International, describes how the Democratizing Data Search and Discovery Platform is making great strides in helping meet the mandates of the Foundations for Evidence-Based Policymaking Act of 2018 (Evidence Act) and demonstrating the value of federal data. The act encourages federal agencies to improve the way they use data for evidence-based decision-making. It emphasizes the importance of data sharing, data transparency, and data accessibility across government agencies. To discover who is using federal data, the search and discovery platform allows agencies to track metrics through machine learning and language models to gain a deeper understanding of use. This column is an introduction to a deeper dive exploring the collection and value of usage data that will be published in a special HDSR issue, "Democratizing Data: Data as a Public Asset," planned for spring 2024.

Democratizing Our Data: A Manifesto

Julia Lane
Public data are foundational to our democratic system. People need consistently high-quality information from trustworthy sources. In the new economy, wealth is generated by access to data; government's job is to democratize the data playing field. Yet data produced by the American government are getting worse and costing more. In Democratizing Our Data, Julia Lane argues that good data are essential for democracy. Her book is a wake-up call to America to fix its broken public data system.

Data Inventories for the Modern Age? Using Data Science to Open Government Data

Julia Lane, Ernesto Gimeno, Ekaterina Levitskaya, Zheyuan Zhang, and Alberto Zigoni
This article describes how data science techniques—machine learning and natural language processing—can be used to open the black box of government data. It then describes how an incentive structure can be established—using human–computer interaction techniques —to create a new and sustainable data ecosystem. The particular focus is on the United States and on scientific researchers, who are major users of government data. However, the approach can be deployed to other use cases, such as data mentions in newspapers and government reports, and many other countries.

Rich Search and Discovery for Research Datasets

Edited by Julia Lane, Ian Mulvany, and Paco Nathan
This ground-breaking book explores how automating the search for and discovery of datasets can help tackle irreproducibility in social science.

Show US the Data Podcast Series

Nancy Potok
The Show US the Data podcast series is a series of six five-minute podcasts, discussing a variety of data sets from agencies that participated in the Show US the Data pilot study.

Toward Democratizing Access to Facilities Data: A Framework for Intelligent Data Discovery and Delivery

Yubo Qin, Ivan Rodero, and Manish Parashar (2022)
This paper presents a comprehensive framework for intelligent data discovery and delivery to democratize access to large-scale scientific facility data. The authors explore how state-of-the-art techniques for data discovery and access can be adapted to facilitate data access and develop a conceptual framework that includes user query analysis, optimized data caching and prefetching mechanisms, and a collaborative knowledge-aware graph-attention network (CKAT) recommendation model. The framework addresses challenges in accessing data from large-scale instruments, observatories, and sensor networks by leveraging machine learning and content delivery network technologies.

National Artificial Intelligence Research Resource (NAIRR): Preliminary Findings and Recommendations

Office of Science and Technology Policy (OSTP) (2022)
The NAIRR represents a vision for a shared national research infrastructure for responsible discovery and innovation in AI. Led by the U.S. National Science Foundation in partnership with 12 other federal agencies and 26 non-governmental partners, the NAIRR pilot brings together computational, data, software, model, training and user support resources to demonstrate and investigate all major elements of the NAIRR vision. The initiative aims to democratize access to AI resources and tools for the broad research and education communities while advancing trustworthy AI and protecting privacy, civil rights and civil liberties.

Strengthening and Democratizing the U.S. Artificial Intelligence Innovation Ecosystem

Office of Science and Technology Policy (2023)
This comprehensive report from the NAIRR Task Force presents the final recommendations for implementing a National Artificial Intelligence Research Resource. The report outlines a phased approach for establishing a shared national cyberinfrastructure that would democratize access to computational resources, high-quality datasets, educational tools, and user support needed for AI research and development. The task force's recommendations address governance models, security frameworks, sustainability plans, and the importance of ensuring broad, equitable access to AI research infrastructure across the research community.

Toward Democratizing Access to Science Data: Introducing the National Data Platform

Manish Parashar and Ilkay Altintas (2023)
This paper introduces the National Data Platform (NDP), a project aimed at catalyzing an open, equitable and extensible data ecosystem for scientific research. The authors discuss the importance and urgency of open and equitable data access, explore barriers and challenges to such access, and present the vision and architecture of the NDP. The platform is designed to bridge data gaps for AI by offering an online ecosystem of data services that enables researchers, educators, and students to discover, access, and use AI-ready data while executing AI workflows and deploying distributed AI services.

Data Democratization: An Ecosystemic Contemplation and Coordination

Xiao-Li Meng (2024)
In this Harvard Data Science Review editorial, Meng explores the concept of data democratization from an ecosystemic perspective, drawing parallels between democratic governance and data ecosystem management. The article discusses the importance of balancing forces in data ecosystems and emphasizes the need for public counterparts to private data collection and control. Meng argues that democratizing data becomes essential due to the boom of data science and generative AI, highlighting the need for sustainable equilibrium in data access and governance to benefit society as a whole.

Enabling Responsible Artificial Intelligence Research and Development Through the Democratization of Advanced Cyberinfrastructure

Manish Parashar (2024)
This article explores the critical importance of democratizing AI research and development to achieve responsible AI outcomes. Parashar discusses how advances in AI R&D are often tied to access to large amounts of computational power and data, highlighting the need for widely accessible AI research cyberinfrastructure. The paper introduces the National Data Platform as a solution for catalyzing an open, equitable, and extensible data ecosystem that can help democratize the AI R&D landscape while promoting principles of fairness, accountability, transparency, and equity.

NAIRR Pilot Demonstration: Integrating Democratizing Data Services into the National Data Platform

National Science Foundation (2024)
This NSF EAGER project demonstrates the integration of democratizing data services into the National Data Platform as part of the NAIRR pilot initiative. The project bridges data gaps for AI by offering an online data ecosystem that enables researchers, educators, and students to discover, access, and use AI-ready data. The platform promotes collaboration and innovation while enhancing existing research computing investments and facilitating scientific and AI advancements. The initiative particularly benefits communities that may not otherwise participate widely in AI research and development.

Make Data Count Resources

Make Data Count Initiative
Make Data Count is a comprehensive initiative that promotes open data metrics to enable the evaluation and reward of research data reuse and impact. The resource collection includes evidence from bibliometric studies, webinars, presentations, and practical guidelines for recognizing data and software contributions in academic evaluation. The initiative emphasizes the importance of contextualizing data metrics according to usage patterns in different disciplines and provides tools and frameworks for making responsible data metrics a reality in scholarly communication and research evaluation.
