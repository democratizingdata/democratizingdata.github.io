DASHBOARD OVERVIEW

Understanding USDA Data Impact

The USDA Dashboard provides comprehensive insights into how data assets from the U.S. Department of Agriculture are used in published research. Our machine learning algorithms analyze research publications to identify dataset citations, research applications, and impact metrics that support evidence-based agricultural and food policy making.

Key Research Areas

  • Agricultural Economics: Farm income, production costs, market analysis
  • Food Security: Access, availability, and nutrition research
  • Rural Development: Community economic development studies
  • Environmental Sustainability: Conservation and climate research
  • Trade and Markets: International agricultural trade analysis
  • Technology Adoption: Innovation in agricultural practices
  • Policy Analysis: Impact of agricultural policies
  • Demographics: Rural population and labor studies

Featured USDA Datasets

Census of Agriculture

Comprehensive data on farms, ranches, and agricultural production across the United States.

Agricultural Resource Management Survey (ARMS)

Detailed information on farm business and household characteristics, production practices, and resource use.

Food Access Research Atlas

Mapping tool providing spatial overview of food access indicators for low-income and other census tracts.

Rural-Urban Continuum Codes

Classification scheme that distinguishes metropolitan counties by population size and nonmetropolitan counties by degree of urbanization.

About This Dashboard

The USDA Dashboard is part of the Democratizing Data Initiative's effort to enable different communities to understand how government data assets are used. The dashboard draws from a database that describes how USDA data assets are referenced in research publications, linking mentions with research topics, authors, and their affiliated institutions.

Note: This pilot project reflects information associated with datasets specifically requested for analysis and is not intended to capture all references to all USDA datasets. The project continues through America's Datahub Consortium.
