DASHBOARD OVERVIEW

Understanding how Food and Agricultural Research data are used

The Food and Agricultural Research Dashboard provides new information about how food and agricultural research data are used in scientific research. The search process, described in more detail in the 2024 Harvard Science Review special issue and the Technical Report, identifies mentions of datasets in publications, to find what authors from which institutions have studied what research areas using each dataset. This information can be used by researchers to find other experts to build a common community of practice, by data producers to make their data more visible and usable, and by the public to get a better sense of how research data have been used.

This is a beta version. More food and agricultural research datasets could readily be added as the community identifies datasets of interest. Improved search tools could also be developed.

Key Research Areas

  • Agricultural Economics: Farm income, production costs, market analysis
  • Food Security: Access, availability, and nutrition research
  • Rural Development: Community economic development studies
  • Environmental Sustainability: Conservation and climate research
  • Trade and Markets: International agricultural trade analysis
  • Technology Adoption: Innovation in agricultural practices
  • Policy Analysis: Impact of agricultural policies
  • Demographics: Rural population and labor studies

Featured Food And Agricultural Research Datasets

US Department of Agriculture Census of Agriculture

Comprehensive data on farms, ranches, and agricultural production across the United States.

Agricultural Resource Management Survey (ARMS)

Detailed information on farm business and household characteristics, production practices, and resource use.

Food Access Research Atlas

Mapping tool providing spatial overview of food access indicators for low-income and other census tracts.

Rural-Urban Continuum Codes

Classification scheme that distinguishes metropolitan counties by population size and nonmetropolitan counties by degree of urbanization.

About This Beta Dashboard

Beta Version Notice: This is a beta version of the Food And Agricultural Research Dashboard. We are actively working on validating the collected metadata and improving our data analysis methods. The results shown should be considered preliminary as we continue to refine our approach.

The Food And Agricultural Research Dashboard is part of the Democratizing Data Initiative's effort to enable different communities to understand how government data assets are used. The dashboard draws from a database that describes how Food And Agricultural Research data assets are referenced in research publications, linking mentions with research topics, authors, and their affiliated institutions.

Important: This pilot project reflects information associated with datasets specifically requested for analysis and is not intended to capture all references to all Food And Agricultural Research datasets. As this is a beta version, we are continuously working to validate and improve the accuracy of our metadata collection and analysis processes. The project continues through America's Datahub Consortium.
