Workforce & Skills Data Usage Dashboard

Discover how workforce development and skills datasets are utilized in labor market research, education studies, and human capital development publications.

Understanding Workforce Data Impact

The Workforce & Skills Dashboard provides comprehensive insights into how federal workforce and education datasets are used in published research. Our analysis connects mentions of these critical data sources with research on employment dynamics, skills development, and education outcomes to support evidence-based workforce policy.

Key Research Domains

  • Labor Market Dynamics: Employment flows, job transitions, wage analysis
  • Skills Assessment: Occupational competencies and requirements
  • Education Outcomes: Post-secondary employment and earnings
  • Workforce Development: Training program effectiveness
  • Regional Economics: Geographic employment patterns
  • Human Capital: Skills matching and talent development
  • Policy Evaluation: Workforce program impact assessment
  • Career Pathways: Educational and occupational mobility

Longitudinal Employer-Household Dynamics (LEHD)

Comprehensive data on employment, earnings, and job flows at detailed geographic and industry levels. Includes Quarterly Workforce Indicators (QWI), Job-to-Job Flows (J2J), and Origin-Destination Employment Statistics (LODES).

Unemployment Insurance (UI) Wage Records

State-level administrative data providing detailed earnings histories and employment patterns for workforce development and economic analysis.

State Longitudinal Data Systems (SLDS)

Integrated data systems linking student information across the education and workforce spectrum, from early childhood through post-secondary education and into the workforce.

Program for International Assessment of Adult Competencies (PIAAC)

International assessment measuring adults' skills in literacy, numeracy, and problem-solving in technology-rich environments.

O*NET Occupational Information Network

Comprehensive database of worker attributes and job characteristics, including skills, abilities, knowledge, work activities, and work context for hundreds of occupations.

Occupational Requirements Survey (ORS)

Provides data on the cognitive and physical requirements of occupations in the U.S. economy, supporting vocational rehabilitation and job placement.

About This Dashboard

The Workforce & Skills Dashboard is part of the Democratizing Data Initiative's comprehensive effort to track how federal data investments support research and decision-making. By analyzing millions of research publications, we identify how workforce and education datasets are used to advance understanding of human capital development and labor market dynamics.

Note: This dashboard represents a pilot analysis of selected workforce and skills datasets and their research applications. Coverage focuses on demonstrating methodology and providing insights into data usage patterns that can inform future investments in workforce data infrastructure.
